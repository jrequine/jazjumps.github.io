December Show & Getting DJ Competition Ready

With the Suffolk Equestrian Christmas Show Jumping just around the corner, the focus right now is all about getting DJ back into competition shape. After a quieter autumn, it's time to ramp things up and start building towards our spring goals.

Why This Show Matters

December's show at Suffolk Equestrian might seem like just another local unaffiliated competition, but for us it's much more than that. It's the first step in what's going to be a demanding 2026 season. We'll be competing at 1.00m and 1.10m‚Äînothing too ambitious, but enough to get DJ thinking, moving, and back into the rhythm of competition.

There's no substitute for actually getting out there and competing. Building Fitness for Spring

The real goal here is building DJ's fitness and sharpness heading into the spring. With the Andalucia Sunshine Tour in March, we need to arrive in Spain with a horse that's fit, confident, and competition-ready. That doesn't happen overnight‚Äîit's built through consistent work over the winter months.

Our training right now is focused on:

  • Cardiovascular fitness through varied flatwork and hacking
  • Gymnastic exercises to keep him supple and adjustable
  • Low-key jumping sessions to maintain his technique without overdoing it
  • Plenty of variety to keep him mentally fresh

It's a balance between doing enough to build fitness and not doing so much that we peak too early or risk injury.
